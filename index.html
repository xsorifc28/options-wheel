Wheel Coach

Step-by-step logic for the options wheel.

Phase Action Price Entry Premium Cash Flow
01
The Setup: You're selling a Put. You are telling the market "I will buy this stock at $Strike if it falls." They pay you a "Premium" just for making that promise.
Sell Put +$164.00
02
The Trap: The stock fell! You now own 100 shares. Your Effective Basis is the Strike minus the Premium you already kept. You aren't "losing" money, you're just holding an asset.
Assigned Basis:
$265.86		 Asset Acquired
03
Extraction: Now you sell a "Covered Call." You are charging "rent" to hold the stock. By defaulting to your Basis, you ensure that if the stock is sold, you don't lose a cent of your original capital.
Sell Call +$50.00
04
The Exit: The stock was "Called Away." You sold it at your Call Strike. Because the Call Strike was lower than your original Put Strike, it shows as a "Loss" on the stock flip—but the premiums cover it.
Called Away Flip Result:
-$164.00		 -$164.00
05
The Result: You finished the wheel! This number is your Total Net Gain. It is the sum of every premium collected, minus any loss (or plus any gain) from the stock movement.
Cycle Total $214.00
